What is Flint?

Flint is a non-profit organization? that seeks to bridge academic and experiential education by connecting students with established entrepreneurs who will provide them with both guidance and real-world challenges.

At its core, Flint is a mentorship program between Cornellian students and experienced professionals and/or Cornell alumni. Flint is unique and highly accessible in that it provides a convenient, interactive online platform upon which students and employers can communicate with each other.




Students will gain hands-on experience working on real-world problems, with established professionals. They will not only obtain mentorship from adept and knowledgeable entrepreneurs in the industry in which they are interested, but also have the opportunity to join mentors' startups as interns or full-time employees.

For Students

For Mentors

By tapping into a vast talent pool of Cornellians, mentors can accelerate their talent discovery process. Flint will allow mentors to learn about students in great depth, and thus increase the efficiency and effectivity of their hiring decisions. Online and offline interaction with students will also gives mentors an avenue towards broader visibility on the Cornell campus.




In scientific terms, flint is a hard, sedimentary cryptocrystalline form of the mineral, quartz. In layman's terms, flint is a rock. But, it's no ordinary rock; under the right circumstances, flint edge can produce sparks which ignite fires.

Our vision at Flint is to be a spark, or a catalyst, for the enhancement of students' education, experience, and career opportunities. We want to initiate a lifelong flame and dedication towards tackling real-world business problems within students, by first, striking a match between students and experienced mentors.
